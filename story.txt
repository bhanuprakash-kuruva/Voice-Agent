In a quiet village by a river, three unlikely friends met every evening under an old banyan tree.

Ravi was curious and always asking questions. Meera loved drawing the world around her. And Kiran—he never spoke much, but he noticed everything.

One day, the river began to dry. The villagers worried, but no one knew why.

Ravi explored upstream and found fallen branches blocking the flow. Meera sketched the problem clearly so others could understand. Kiran silently gathered tools and started clearing the debris.

Together, they restored the river.

That evening, as water flowed again, Ravi smiled, Meera drew the shining stream, and Kiran simply sat—watching everything, just as always.

And from that day on, the village knew: sometimes, it takes curiosity, creativity, and quiet action to solve a problem.
